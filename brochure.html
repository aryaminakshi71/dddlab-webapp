Complete Veterinary Laboratory Testing — Gurugram, Haryana

The District Disease Diagnosis Laboratory, Gurugram is now equipped with a high-precision visible spectrophotometer capable of performing 145+ biochemical, toxicological, nutritional and quality-control tests on serum, blood, urine, milk, feed, water and meat samples from all livestock species.


All veterinary surgeons in the district are requested to utilise these diagnostic services for disease confirmation, treatment monitoring, metabolic profiling, nutritional assessment, and toxicology investigations in cattle, buffalo, sheep, goats, horses, dogs, cats and poultry.

⚠️ Aflatoxin Screening in Feed & Milk

Aflatoxin B1 in feed can be screened at 360nm by solvent extraction method. Aflatoxin M1 in milk (carry-over from contaminated feed) can also be detected. This is critical for dairy herds showing liver disease, immunosuppression or ill-thrift. Safe limit: Aflatoxin B1 <10 ppb in feed (India). Confirmatory testing by TLC or ELISA recommended for positive cases.

Note on Meat Species ID

Spectrophotometric method gives PRESUMPTIVE identification based on myoglobin content. Buffalo meat has significantly higher myoglobin than cattle. For legal/forensic confirmation, PCR-DNA analysis is the gold standard.

Sample Requirements

Serum/Plasma: 2–5 ml blood in plain tube. Centrifuge before sending.
Urine: Fresh mid-stream urine 5–10 ml in clean container.
Milk: Fresh milk 10–20 ml in clean bottle.
Feed/Fodder: 100–200g representative sample in sealed bag.
Water: 500 ml in clean plastic bottle.
Meat: Fresh 50–100g sample in sealed bag with ice.
